FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I have wasps or hornets?

Hornets are generally larger than most wasps and often build large aerial nests. A professional inspection can accurately identify the species.

Is it safe to remove a wasp nest myself?

Removing a wasp or hornet nest can be dangerous, especially if the colony is large or located near occupied areas. Professional removal is recommended.

When are wasps most active?

Wasp activity typically increases throughout spring and summer, with colonies reaching peak size in late summer and early fall.

Can bees damage my home?

Certain species, such as carpenter bees, can cause damage by tunneling into wood structures.

What should I do if I find a nest on my property?

Avoid disturbing the nest and contact a professional for inspection and treatment recommendations.

Will wasps return after nest removal?

New colonies may establish in the future if favorable nesting conditions remain. Preventative measures can help reduce this risk.

How quickly can a nest be removed?

Treatment timing depends on nest size, location, and species involved. Most inspections can determine the best removal approach quickly.

Do you serve areas outside Worcester?

Yes. We provide wasp, hornet, and bee removal services throughout Worcester and surrounding Worcester County communities.
