Understanding Bed Bugs

Bed bugs are small, flat, reddish-brown insects approximately the size of an apple seed. They feed on human blood at night while people sleep and spend the rest of their time hidden in mattress seams, box spring folds, bed frames, headboards, baseboards, electrical outlets, nightstands, and other tight spaces near sleeping areas.

Signs of a bed bug infestation include small blood spots on sheets, tiny dark fecal spots on mattresses, shed nymph skins, and in heavier infestations, a sweet musty odor. Because reactions vary and bed bugs hide so effectively, infestations often go undetected until populations are well-established.

Our Treatment Process

  • Detailed inspection of all mattresses, frames, furniture, and void spaces.
  • Professional-grade residual insecticides applied to cracks, crevices, and harborage areas.
  • High-temperature steam treatment to kill bugs and eggs on contact without chemicals.
  • Professional dust formulations for long-term protection in wall voids.
  • Biological Bed Bug Solution available for Pro-Active situations (3-month residual).

Bed Bug Prevention Tips for Worcester Residents

Inspect sleeping areas regularly for early signs of activity, especially if you travel frequently, purchase used furniture, or live in a multi-unit building. Encase mattresses and box springs in certified bed bug-proof covers. When traveling, inspect hotel rooms before unpacking and keep luggage away from beds and floors. Avoid bringing used furniture or mattresses into your home without thorough inspection. In apartment buildings, seal cracks and gaps around walls and utility penetrations to prevent bed bugs from spreading between units.

Seasonal Guide

Worcester Pest Activity by Season — What to Expect and When

Spring

As temperatures rise, ant colonies emerge. Ticks and mosquitoes become active. Stinging insects construct new nests. Spring is the ideal time for preventive perimeter treatments.

Summer

Peak activity for mosquitoes, yellow jackets, and wasps. Flies become problematic around food service areas. Carpenter ants trail through kitchens. Bed bug activity increases.

Fall

As temperatures drop, mice and rats seek warm shelter inside homes and businesses. Overwintering insects attempt to enter through cracks. Critical season for rodent exclusion.

Winter

Rodents established inside walls remain active, breeding and causing damage. Cockroaches remain active year-round indoors. Winter service visits maintain protective barriers.
